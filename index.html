Travel Tips for Iceland

Transportation

There are tours that you can book in Iceland that will provide transportation by bus, with preset destinations. This may be a valid option if you are travelling alone and want to meet knew people. This will also the eliminate the need to rent a car and pay for gas, which can be very expensive without friends to split the cost.

If travelling with friends, renting a car is a great option for getting around the country. Gas prices are high here, but splitting costs with one or more friends will make it manageable. Driving is very straightforward here, with one major highway going around the majority of the country. This will give you the freedom to visit the locations you prefer, and on your own schedule.

Food

Going out to eat in Iceland can be very expensive. The country has to import the majority of its food, as land conditions prevent them from producing a lot of food of their own. You should be able to finder cheaper food prices at grocery stores, but restaurants will empty your wallet very quickly. It is advised to visit grocery stores to supply at least some of your food, and maybe try a few restaurants that peak your interest.

Places to see

Rejkjavik

Rejkjavik is Iceland's major city, however it is still only a small town. Downtown is very accessible, and is probably best traversed on foot. There are many restaurants and shops to visit here, as well some nice parks, and the water front. While the city is certainly not as stunning as some of the nature you will see in the rest of the country, it is still worth at least a few hours to wander, if you can spare the time, as you will likely be passing through anyway coming from the airport.

The Golden Circle

The Golden Circle is an excellent day trip that contains several great locations to stop and enjoy. It is very easy to access, as you can start and end the loop in Rejkjavik. Along the journey, you can stop and see several breathtaking waterfalls, a hiking trail with interesting rock formations from techtonic shifts, as well as a natural geysir that erupts every 5 to 10 minutes.

Vestmannayjar Island

The island of Vestmannayjar lies just south of the mainland, and will take a bit more planning to travel to, however it is well worth the journey. From the south of the mainland, you will need to take a roughly 30 minute ferry ride down to the island. You are also able to ferry your car across, which is recommended if you want to see the full island. The town in the center of the island is very small, but the outskirts of the island contains several mountains and volcanoes that you can hike, as well as several beaches on the outer edges that you can enjoy.
